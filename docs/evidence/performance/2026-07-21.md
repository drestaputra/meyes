## Scope

MediaPipe Face and Hand Landmarker adapters sequentially on a single 640×480 all-zero in-memory BGR
frame. This is a bounded implementation diagnostic, not live-camera performance evidence.

## Decision

No performance optimization was introduced from this result:

- Face initialization is the largest measured one-time value, but production constructs that
  backend in its dedicated worker rather than on the Qt UI thread.
- Blank-frame warm Face timing did not expose a measured adapter bottleneck.
- Blank-frame warm Hand timing remained below its existing 100 ms scheduling interval at the
  configured 10 FPS worker cadence.
- Changing model options, worker cadence, or native delegates from an all-zero frame would be an
  unsupported optimization and could harm live detection behavior.

The useful action was to retain the repeatable probe and existing latest-frame/cadence boundaries.
Optimize only after representative live measurements identify a bottleneck.

## Honest limitations

- An all-zero frame contains no detected face or hand and cannot measure detection-path cost.
- Sequential adapter timing is not the production concurrency pattern; the production workers are
  independent.
- The result is specific to this host, load, caches, Python/runtime, dependency versions, and power
  state.
- No webcam, real user, end-to-end preview latency, accuracy, sustained thermal behavior, or
  calibrated pointer reach was measured.
- No GUI, emergency hotkey, or operating-system input was activated.
- MediaPipe dependency network behavior remains subject to `PRIVACY.md`.
